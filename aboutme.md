# About

I am a **Research Fellow** at the **Peter Doherty Institute for Infection and Immunity**, **The University of Melbourne**, where I develop computational approaches to understand how microbial communities influence human health and disease.

My research lies at the interface of **microbiology**, **systems biology**, and **bioinformatics**. I am interested in translating large-scale microbiome datasets into biological insight by integrating metagenomics, genome-scale metabolic modelling, statistical modelling, and multi-omics data. My work aims to uncover the functional mechanisms linking microbial communities to disease progression, therapeutic response, and host health.

---

## Research Interests

My current research focuses on:

- Microbial metabolism
- Genome-scale metabolic modelling
- Functional metagenomics
- Multi-omics data integration
- Cancer immunotherapy
- Computational microbiology

## Career

I completed my PhD in Bioinformatics at **Academia Sinica, Taiwan**, where my research focused on understanding microbial community function using systems biology and genome-scale metabolic modelling.

Following my PhD, I joined the **University of Melbourne**, where my research expanded into microbiome systems biology and microbial metabolic modelling. My work has since evolved towards understanding host-associated microbiomes through large-scale metagenomic analyses, functional annotation, and computational modelling.

I am currently a **Research Fellow** at the **Peter Doherty Institute for Infection and Immunity**, where I investigate how microbial metabolism influences therapeutic response and disease progression, with a particular focus on cancer immunotherapy.

---

## Research Philosophy

Microbiomes are extraordinarily diverse, yet understanding which organisms are present is only the first step. My research seeks to answer a more fundamental question:

> **What are microbial communities doing, and how do those functions influence their host?**

By integrating computational and systems biology approaches, I aim to identify functional mechanisms rather than descriptive associations, enabling a deeper understanding of microbial ecology and its applications to human health.
