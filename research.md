# Research
git status
My research focuses on understanding how microbial communities influence human health and disease through computational and systems biology. By integrating metagenomics, genome-scale metabolic modelling, statistical modelling, and multi-omics data, I investigate the functional mechanisms that underpin host–microbiome interactions and microbial community behaviour.

Rather than asking *which microbes are present*, my research seeks to answer a more fundamental question:

> **What are microbial communities doing, and how do those functions influence their host?**

---

## Research Themes

### Microbiome and Human Health

The human microbiome plays a critical role in shaping immune function, metabolism, and disease. My research investigates how microbial communities influence clinical outcomes, with a particular focus on cancer immunotherapy.

Current research includes:

- Gut microbiome and cancer immunotherapy
- Host–microbiome interactions
- Functional biomarkers of therapeutic response
- Clinical microbiome studies
- Translational microbiome research

---

### Microbial Systems Biology

Microbial communities are highly interconnected ecosystems whose behaviour cannot be understood through taxonomy alone. I develop and apply systems biology approaches to investigate microbial metabolism and community function.

Current research includes:

- Genome-scale metabolic modelling
- Community metabolic modelling
- Microbial metabolism
- Functional pathway analysis
- Network-based analysis of microbial communities

---

### Functional Metagenomics and Multi-omics Integration

Modern microbiome studies generate large amounts of genomic and molecular data. My research develops computational workflows that integrate multiple data types to uncover functional relationships between microbial communities and their hosts.

Current research includes:

- Metagenomic analysis
- Functional annotation
- Multi-omics integration
- Comparative microbiome analysis
- Statistical modelling of microbiome data

---

## Research Philosophy

Microbiome research is increasingly moving beyond descriptive studies towards understanding biological mechanisms. My goal is to develop computational approaches that connect microbial genes, pathways, and metabolic functions with clinically meaningful outcomes.

By integrating systems biology with microbiome science, I aim to provide mechanistic insights that advance our understanding of host–microbiome interactions and support the development of microbiome-informed diagnostics and therapeutics.
